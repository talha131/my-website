The Rise of Renewable Energy and Its Global Impact

## Objective

Design an infographic that shows the significant growth of renewable energy sources worldwide, from the early 2000s to the present. Cover key advances in technology, shifts in global energy policies, and the environmental and economic effects of adopting renewable energy.

## Instructions

### Background Research

- Review the history of renewable energy, focusing on solar, wind, hydroelectric, and geothermal power. Include bioenergy if relevant to your scope.
- Look at the technological breakthroughs that have made renewable energy more practical and cost-effective.
- Explore global initiatives and policy changes that have encouraged the adoption of renewable energy.

### Key Elements to Include

- Outline the major technological innovations in renewable energy, showing how they've increased efficiency and reduced costs.
- Present a timeline of significant policy shifts or international agreements that have driven growth in renewable energy usage.
- Cover the environmental benefits, such as reductions in carbon emissions and conservation of natural resources. Also cover economic impacts, including job creation and energy independence.

### Designing Your Infographics

- Use [Canva](https://www.canva.com/) to design your infographic. Choose a template that supports timelines, charts, and maps.
- Use imagery and symbols related to the sun, wind, water, and earth to represent different types of renewable energy. A green and blue color palette works well for this topic.
- Arrange the content to start with an overview of the rise of renewable energy, then move into sections on technological advances, policy changes, and impacts.
- Design the infographic so it is accessible to readers with limited knowledge of renewable energy.
