## Objective

Create a labeled diagram infographic in Canva showing the parts of a plant and what each part does.

## Instructions

### Parts to Label

Draw or place an illustration of a full plant in the center of your canvas. Add callout labels for each of the following parts:

- **Roots**
  - Absorb water and dissolved minerals from the soil.
  - Anchor the plant in the ground.
  - Some roots store food (e.g., carrots, radishes).

- **Stem**
  - Supports the plant and keeps it upright.
  - Transports water and nutrients from roots to leaves (through xylem).
  - Transports food made in the leaves to the rest of the plant (through phloem).

- **Leaves**
  - The main site of photosynthesis - making food using sunlight, water, and CO₂.
  - Gas exchange through tiny pores called stomata (CO₂ in, O₂ out).
  - Transpiration: water evaporates through stomata, pulling more water up from the roots.

- **Flowers**
  - The reproductive part of the plant.
  - Attract pollinators (bees, butterflies) using color and scent.
  - After pollination, the flower develops into a fruit containing seeds.

- **Fruit and Seeds**
  - Fruit protects the seeds.
  - Seeds are dispersed by animals, wind, or water to grow new plants.

### Optional: Cross-Section of a Leaf

Add a small inset diagram showing a cross-section of a leaf with labels for: upper epidermis, palisade mesophyll (where most photosynthesis happens), spongy mesophyll, lower epidermis, and stomata.

### Design in Canva

- Large plant illustration in the center.
- Callout lines pointing from each part to a labeled box.
- Use green as the main color, with a different accent for flowers and fruit.
- Keep each label box to two or three lines of text.

### Required Elements

- Five main parts labeled with function.
- Callout lines clearly pointing to the correct part.
- Optional leaf cross-section as a bonus element.
- Title: "Parts of a Plant and Their Functions."
