## Objective

Create a concept map in Canva that maps the organs of the human digestive system, what each one does, and how food moves through the body from mouth to waste.

## Instructions

### Central Concept

Place **"Human Digestive System"** at the center.

### Main Branches - One Per Organ

Organize your map by organ, in the order food travels:

- **Mouth:** Mechanical digestion through chewing; saliva begins breaking down starch (enzyme: amylase)
- **Esophagus:** Muscular tube that moves food to the stomach via peristalsis (wave-like contractions)
- **Stomach:** Churns food into a paste; hydrochloric acid kills bacteria; pepsin breaks down protein
- **Small Intestine:** Main site of digestion and absorption; enzymes from the pancreas and bile from the liver complete digestion; villi absorb nutrients into the blood
- **Large Intestine:** Absorbs water from remaining material; forms solid waste
- **Liver:** Produces bile, which breaks down fats in the small intestine
- **Pancreas:** Produces digestive enzymes (lipase for fats, protease for protein, amylase for starch) and sends them to the small intestine

### Design in Canva

- Arrange the organs in the order food passes through them, either clockwise or top to bottom.
- Highlight the key enzyme or function in a colored tag on each branch.
- Add a simple body outline showing approximate organ positions.

### Required Elements

- All 7 organs listed above.
- Function of each organ clearly stated.
- At least one enzyme named for each relevant organ.
- Title: "Human Digestive System."
