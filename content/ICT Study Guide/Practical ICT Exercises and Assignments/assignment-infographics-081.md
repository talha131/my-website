## Objective

Create a fishbone diagram in Canva that maps the causes of why students lose focus and get distracted during class.

## What is a Fishbone Diagram?

Look up "fishbone diagram" before starting. It is a cause-and-effect diagram shaped like a fish skeleton. The main problem is at the right, and causes branch off the central spine in groups.

## Instructions

### Step 1: Define the Problem

Write this at the head of the diagram:

**"Students get distracted in the classroom"**

### Step 2: Identify Cause Categories

Use these as your main bones:

- **Technology:** Phone notifications, social media during class, using headphones
- **Physical Environment:** Noise from outside, uncomfortable seating, poor ventilation or lighting
- **Peer Behavior:** Side conversations, friends sitting nearby, group disturbances
- **Teaching Style:** Long lectures with no interaction, monotone delivery, no student participation built in
- **Personal Factors:** Lack of sleep, hunger, personal worries, low interest in the topic

### Step 3: Add Sub-causes

For each category, add at least two or three specific sub-causes. Draw on your own classroom experience.

### Step 4: Build in Canva

- Use a fishbone template in Canva, or build the structure with lines and text boxes.
- Problem on the right. Categories along the spine. Sub-causes on shorter connecting branches.
- Assign a different color to each main category.

### Design Tips

- Keep each label short.
- Use the horizontal layout for clarity.
- Add a relevant icon next to each category label (e.g., a phone icon for Technology, a group icon for Peer Behavior).

### Required Elements

- Problem statement at the fish head.
- At least 5 cause categories with 2 or more sub-causes each.
- Icons next to the main category labels.
- Color-coded branches.
