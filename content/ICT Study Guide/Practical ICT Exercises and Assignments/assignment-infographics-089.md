## Objective

Create a concept map in Canva that explains photosynthesis - the process plants use to make food - showing its inputs, outputs, location, and the equation.

## Instructions

### Central Concept

Place **"Photosynthesis"** at the center.

### Main Branches

- **Definition:** The process by which plants use sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide to produce glucose and oxygen.

- **Inputs:**
  - Sunlight (energy source)
  - Carbon dioxide (CO₂) absorbed from the air through stomata
  - Water (H₂O) absorbed through the roots

- **Outputs:**
  - Glucose (C₆H₁₂O₆) - stored as chemical energy for the plant
  - Oxygen (O₂) - released into the air

- **Location in the Plant:**
  - Takes place in chloroplasts
  - Found mainly in leaf cells
  - Chlorophyll (the green pigment in chloroplasts) absorbs sunlight

- **The Equation:**
  - 6CO₂ + 6H₂O + light energy → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂

- **Why It Matters:**
  - Produces food for the plant
  - Releases the oxygen that animals and humans breathe
  - Forms the base of most food chains

### Design in Canva

- Place the equation in a prominent box at the top or bottom of the map.
- Use green as the main color to match the plant theme.
- Add a simple leaf diagram showing where chloroplasts are located.
- Use arrows to show the flow: inputs entering the leaf, outputs leaving it.

### Required Elements

- Inputs, outputs, and location as separate branches.
- The photosynthesis equation clearly shown.
- A leaf diagram or icon.
- Title: "Photosynthesis."
