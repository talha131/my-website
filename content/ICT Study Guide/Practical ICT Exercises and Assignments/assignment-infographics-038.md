Common allergy types and triggers.

## Objective

Create an infographic about common allergy types and triggers.

## Instructions

- Research common allergy types and their triggers: seasonal allergies, food allergies, pet allergies, and environmental allergens.
- Gather data from medical journals, allergy associations, or health organizations.
- Use Canva to design the infographic.
- Plan the layout and color scheme for clarity and visual appeal.

## Background

- Define what allergies are and briefly explain why the immune system reacts to harmless substances.
- Give an overview of the main allergy types: respiratory (pollen, dust mites), food (nuts, shellfish), and skin (latex, insect stings).
- Note how common allergies are and how they affect daily life.

## Key Elements

- **Allergy Types:** Name and describe common types with their main symptoms.
- **Triggers:** Identify the triggers for each type, such as pollen for seasonal allergies or peanuts for food allergies.
- **Symptoms:** List typical reactions - sneezing, itching, hives, and difficulty breathing - so readers know what to look for.
- **Management Tips:** Give practical steps, such as avoiding known triggers and seeking medical attention for severe reactions.

## Presentation

- Use a clear layout with sections and a logical information flow.
- Add icons or illustrations to make each section easy to scan.
- Keep text brief and use bullet points where possible.
- Include statistics to show how widespread allergies are.
- Cite sources so readers can find more information.
