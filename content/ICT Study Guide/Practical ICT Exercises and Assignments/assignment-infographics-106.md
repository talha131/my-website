## Objective

Create a flowchart in Canva that guides a school student through the process of using a library - from entering and finding a book to borrowing and returning it.

## Instructions

### Flowchart Steps

Map the process in this order:

1. **Enter the library** - Be quiet. Sign in if required.
2. **Know what you need** - Do you have a specific title or author? Or are you browsing by subject?
3. **Use the catalogue** - Check the library's catalogue (card catalogue or computer system) to find the book's call number.
4. **Understand the Dewey Decimal System** - Books are organized by subject number. Add a brief reference box showing the 10 main Dewey categories (see below).
5. **Find the shelf** - Go to the section that matches the call number. Books are ordered by number, then alphabetically by author's surname.
6. **Check the book** - Is it the right one? Check the table of contents and introduction.
7. **Borrow the book** - Take it to the librarian or self-checkout. Your library card will be stamped or scanned.
8. **Return on time** - Return before the due date. If you need more time, ask for a renewal.

### Dewey Decimal Reference Box

Include a small reference panel inside your infographic showing the 10 main Dewey Decimal classes:

- 000 - General works, Computer science
- 100 - Philosophy and Psychology
- 200 - Religion
- 300 - Social Sciences
- 400 - Language
- 500 - Science
- 600 - Technology
- 700 - Arts and Recreation
- 800 - Literature
- 900 - History and Geography

### Decision Point

After step 2, add a decision diamond: "Do you know the exact title?" → Yes: go to step 3 (catalogue). → No: browse by Dewey subject section directly.

### Design in Canva

- Standard flowchart shapes: rectangles for steps, diamond for decision.
- Numbered steps.
- The Dewey reference box in a separate panel, clearly linked to step 4.

### Required Elements

- All 8 steps in order.
- Decision diamond with two paths.
- Dewey Decimal reference panel.
- Title: "How to Use a Library."
