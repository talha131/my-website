## Objective

Create a fishbone diagram in Canva that identifies why students do not complete homework on time.

## What is a Fishbone Diagram?

Look up "fishbone diagram" before starting. It is also called an Ishikawa or cause-and-effect diagram. The main problem is written at the right (the fish head), and causes branch off the central spine in groups.

## Instructions

### Step 1: Define the Problem

Write this at the head of the diagram:

**"Students do not complete homework on time"**

### Step 2: Identify Cause Categories

Use these as your main bones:

- **Time Management:** No study schedule, procrastination, poor planning
- **Workload:** Too many assignments at once, unclear instructions, not enough class time to understand the task
- **Home Environment:** Household duties, shared devices, no quiet space to work
- **Motivation:** Not seeing the purpose of the task, fear of making mistakes, low interest in the subject
- **Resources:** No internet access, missing class notes, no reference materials at home

### Step 3: Add Sub-causes

For each category, add at least two specific sub-causes based on what you observe among students you know.

### Step 4: Build in Canva

- Search for a fishbone diagram template in Canva, or build it with lines and text boxes.
- Problem statement on the right. Categories along the spine. Sub-causes on shorter branches.
- One color per main category.

### Design Tips

- Write cause labels in short phrases - three to five words each.
- Use arrows pointing toward the spine to show direction.
- Bold the category names so they stand out from sub-causes.

### Required Elements

- Problem statement clearly written at the fish head.
- At least 5 main cause categories.
- At least 2 sub-causes per category.
- Color-coded branches.
