## Objective

Create a Sankey diagram in Canva that maps how you spend a 24-hour day, broken down by activity.

## What is a Sankey Diagram?

Before starting, look up "Sankey diagram" to understand how it works. A Sankey diagram uses bands of varying width to show how a quantity flows and splits. The wider the band, the larger the portion it represents. In this assignment, the quantity is time.

## Instructions

### Step 1: Record Your Day

Track or honestly estimate how you spend a typical 24-hour day. Break it into categories such as:

- Sleep
- Prayer (Fajr, Zuhr, Asr, Maghrib, Isha)
- Study (university classes, self-study, assignments)
- Meals and eating
- Family time
- Travel and commuting
- Screen time (phone, social media, TV)
- Exercise or leisure

Make sure all hours add up to 24.

### Step 2: Organize the Flows

Set up the diagram in two levels:

- **Level 1 (main categories):** Sleep, Productive Time, Personal and Social Time
- **Level 2 (activities):** Break each main category into specific activities

For example, Productive Time might split into: university classes, self-study, and assignment work.

### Step 3: Build in Canva

- Use Canva's diagram elements, or generate the Sankey diagram at sankeymatic.com and import the image into your Canva design.
- Assign a distinct color to each main category.
- Label every flow with the number of hours.
- Title: "How I Spend My 24 Hours."

### Design Tips

- Left-to-right layout works best: total hours on the left, specific activities on the right.
- Use bold, readable fonts for labels.
- Keep the background plain so the flows are easy to follow.

### Required Elements

- All 24 hours accounted for.
- At least 8 activity categories.
- Color-coded flows with labeled hours.
- A clear title.
