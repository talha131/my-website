Pablo Picasso: Innovator of the Art World

## Objective

Create an infographic that covers the life, work, and influence of Pablo Picasso, one of the most well-known figures in 20th-century art. Cover key periods in his career, his contributions to modern art movements, and how his innovations continue to influence artists around the world.

## Instructions

### Background Research

- Read about Picasso's biography, noting significant events that influenced his art, including his early years, the Blue and Rose periods, African-influenced period, Cubism, and later works.
- Explore his role in founding Cubism and his contributions to other art movements.
- Consider the cultural and historical contexts that shaped his work.

### Key Elements to Include

- Include a timeline of Picasso's life, marking his birth, key artistic phases, major works, and his death.
- Cover Picasso's role in developing Cubism and his influence on modern art, including his use of form, color, and perspective.
- Feature some of Picasso's most famous pieces, and explain their significance and impact.
- Discuss how Picasso's work continues to influence contemporary art and culture.
- Tool Selection: Use [Canva](https://www.canva.com/) for your design. Choose a template that supports the integration of images and timelines.
- Visual Elements: Use a color scheme and visual motifs that reflect Picasso's style, particularly those associated with Cubism. Incorporate abstract shapes and fragmented forms to mirror his artistic approach.
- Layout: Arrange the content to provide a chronological view of Picasso's life and work, with separate sections for his contributions to art, notable works, and his place in art history.

### Presentation

- Design the infographic to be educational and accessible, suitable for both students and art enthusiasts who may not be familiar with the full breadth of Picasso's work.
