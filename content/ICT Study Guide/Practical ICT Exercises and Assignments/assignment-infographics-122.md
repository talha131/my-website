## Objective

Create a concept map in Canva that maps what it means to be a responsible digital citizen, covering the key areas students and teachers need to understand when using the internet and digital devices.

## Instructions

### Central Concept

Place **"Digital Citizenship"** at the center.

### Main Branches

- **Online Safety:**
  - Use strong, unique passwords for each account.
  - Enable two-factor authentication.
  - Do not share personal information (home address, phone number, school name) with strangers online.
  - Know what to do if you feel unsafe: tell a trusted adult, block and report.

- **Responsible Use:**
  - Use screen time intentionally - set limits for social media and entertainment.
  - Do not use devices in ways that distract from studying or prayer.
  - Follow your school's rules on phone and device use.

- **Digital Communication:**
  - Be respectful in messages, comments, and online groups.
  - Cyberbullying - sending hurtful messages, spreading rumors, or sharing private images - is harmful and can be illegal.
  - Think before you post: would you be comfortable if your teacher or parent saw this?

- **Privacy:**
  - Whatever you post online can be shared further, even after deletion.
  - Apps and websites collect your data. Read privacy settings and limit what is shared.
  - Protect others' privacy too: do not share photos or information about others without their permission.

- **Copyright and Plagiarism:**
  - Images, music, and text found online belong to someone. Using them without credit is plagiarism.
  - Use Creative Commons or free-to-use resources, or credit the original creator.

- **Identifying Fake News:**
  - Check whether multiple reliable sources report the same story.
  - Look at the website's domain and author credentials.
  - Be careful with forwarded WhatsApp messages - verify before sharing.

### Design in Canva

- Six branches from the center, each in a distinct color.
- Each branch has three to four specific sub-nodes.
- Add relevant icons (a padlock for safety, a speech bubble for communication, a shield for privacy).

### Required Elements

- All six branches with at least 3 sub-nodes each.
- Icons on main branch nodes.
- Title: "Digital Citizenship."
