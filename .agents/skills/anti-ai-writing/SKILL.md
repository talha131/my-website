# **I. CONTENT PATTERNS TO ELIMINATE**

## **1\. Inflated Importance Framing**

**AI constantly upgrades neutral facts into historic milestones.**

### **Common Signals**

- "pivotal moment"
- "key turning point"
- "crucial role"
- "significant shift"
- "testament to"
- "underscores its importance"
- "deeply rooted in"
- "lasting legacy"
- "marks a new era"
- "evolving landscape"

### **Why This Happens**

LLMs regress toward statistically common praise language found in media, marketing, and academic summaries.

### **What To Do Instead**

- State what happened.
- Let the reader decide significance.
- Use measurable outcomes instead of abstract importance.

**Bad (AI-coded):**

The launch marked a pivotal moment in the evolving landscape of digital education.

**Better:**

The platform launched in 2023 and reached 200,000 users in six months.

Specificity beats symbolic framing.

---

## **2\. Generic "Broader Impact" Statements**

AI loves connecting everything to something larger.

### **Watch For:**

- "reflects broader trends"
- "contributes to the wider conversation"
- "highlights the importance of"
- "shaping the future of"
- "symbolizes a shift toward"

### **Why This Happens**

LLMs are trained on explanatory summaries that constantly generalize.

### **What To Do Instead**

Remove any sentence that:

- Could apply to 50 other topics.
- Adds interpretation without new information.

If deleting it changes nothing - it was AI filler.

---

## **3\. Overstated Cultural or Emotional Meaning**

AI injects symbolism where none was asked for.

### **Example Pattern:**

- "This stands as a symbol of resilience."
- "It represents unity and shared purpose."
- "It embodies innovation and transformation."

Unless you're writing literary analysis - cut it.

Humans don't assign metaphor to everything.

---

## **4\. Forced "Challenges & Future Outlook" Sections**

AI loves this rigid structure:

Despite its success, X faces several challenges…
Looking ahead, X is poised to…

### **Why?**

It mirrors textbook templates.

### **Fix:**

End naturally.
Don't manufacture a balanced conclusion.

Real writing stops when the thought ends.

---

# **II. LANGUAGE PATTERNS TO REMOVE**

## **5\. AI Vocabulary Clusters**

LLMs overuse specific words - especially together.

### **High-Frequency AI Words**

Additionally
Crucial
Delve
Highlight
Underscore
Enhance
Foster
Pivotal
Showcase
Intricate
Landscape
Testament
Vibrant
Valuable
Meticulous
Align with
Bolster
Garner
Enduring

One word is fine.

A cluster is a red flag.

### **Why This Matters**

Studies show these words spiked post-2022 in AI-assisted writing.

### **Fix**

Replace with:

- simpler verbs
- plain statements
- concrete nouns

---

## **6\. Avoiding "Is" and "Has"**

AI often replaces simple verbs to sound sophisticated.

### **Instead of:**

- serves as
- features
- offers
- boasts
- represents
- marks

### **Use:**

- is
- has
- does

Basic verbs feel human because humans optimize for clarity, not sophistication.

---

## **7\. Negative Parallelisms**

AI frequently writes:

- "Not only X, but also Y."
- "It's not just…, it's…"
- "This is not…, but rather…"
- "No X, no Y - just Z."

This rhetorical balancing act is statistically common in persuasive writing - so AI overuses it.

Humans use it occasionally.
AI uses it habitually.

Cut it unless it's structurally necessary.

---

## **8\. The Rule of Three Overuse**

AI loves rhythmic triples:

- "innovation, efficiency, and growth"
- "strategy, execution, and impact"
- "bold, dynamic, and transformative"

Once? Fine.
Repeated? Synthetic.

Break rhythm intentionally.

---

# **III. STRUCTURAL TELLS**

## **9\. Slide-Deck List Formatting**

AI defaults to:

1. **Heading:** Explanation
2. **Heading:** Explanation
3. **Heading:** Explanation

It mirrors presentation slides and training manuals.

Vary structure:

- Use paragraphs.
- Combine points.
- Break predictable symmetry.

---

## **10\. No Em Dashes**

Do not use em dashes (—) anywhere. Not for emphasis, not for asides, not for rhetorical pauses.

Use a hyphen (-), a colon (:), or restructure the sentence instead.

AI loves dramatic pauses with em dashes. Humans use them sparingly if at all. In this project, the rule is simple: zero em dashes.

---

## **11\. Emoji Decoration in Explanations**

Especially:

AI uses emoji as structural anchors.

Use them intentionally - not mechanically.

---

# **IV. AUTHORITY & ATTRIBUTION RED FLAGS**

## **12\. Vague Authority References**

"Experts argue…"
"Industry leaders believe…"
"Several publications noted…"
"Widely recognized as…"

If you don't name it, delete it.

AI generalizes attribution to sound credible.

Humans cite specifically or stay silent.

---

## **13\. Knowledge-Cutoff or Source Disclaimers**

Delete:

- "As of my last update…"
- "Based on available information…"
- "Details are not widely documented…"
- "While information is limited…"

These are AI self-protection mechanisms.

Humans don't hedge like that unless legally required.

---

# **V. MARKUP & META PATTERNS (If Copying From AI)**

Remove:

- Markdown formatting artifacts
- Placeholder text like INSERT_URL
- Subject lines ("Subject: …")
- Overly verbose summaries explaining tone adjustments
- Policy disclaimers
- "I hope this helps"
- "Would you like me to…"

AI includes assistant-style communication unless constrained.

---

# **VI. FINAL HUMANIZATION FILTER**

Before publishing, run this test:

### **Delete Every Sentence That:**

- Interprets without evidence.
- Assigns importance abstractly.
- Connects to vague "broader trends."
- Uses 2+ AI vocabulary words.
- Sounds like a keynote speech.

If 20-30% disappears - that's normal.

AI expands. Humans compress.
